Our Story

How we started cooking for others

I started cooking in my mother's kitchen in Nairobi when I was young. She taught me how to make ugali, chapati, and all the dishes we eat at home. When I moved to my own place, neighbors would smell my cooking and ask if I could make extra for them too.

That's how Naggy's Kitchen began - just me cooking for people who missed home-cooked meals. Now we have a small team, but we still cook the same way my mother taught me. No fancy techniques, just good food made with care.

I still go to Marikiti Market myself to choose the best vegetables. I know the farmers by name, and they know what I like. This is important to me because the food we eat should come from people we trust.

What We Do Differently

Kenyan Markets

From Naggy
